Model Note

This simulator is directional. It does not claim audited facility power draw, rack-level telemetry, or vendor-certified cost accounting. It is intended to compare baseline centralized delivery against a convergence-layer scenario under the same workload assumptions.

  • Higher requests per user increases token volume fast — this is the highest-leverage input for total daily load.
  • Larger prompts raise token throughput but can also raise watt pressure on un-cached paths.
  • Edge offload reduces centralized user-path power demand; gains compound with semantic cache.
  • Semantic cache hit rate compresses repeat-token compute — the most efficient watts are the ones never spent.
  • Peak-hour stress penalizes centralized delivery first; the convergence layer absorbs disproportionate benefit.
